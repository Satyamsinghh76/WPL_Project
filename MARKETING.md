## 💼 **For Investors/Accelerators**

### **One-Liner**
Infrastructure for research trust and attention health. Academic GitHub with anti-addictive social design.

### **Problem**
- **$1.5T annually** spent on academic research, but 70% of published findings fail to replicate
- **Knowledge is fragmented**: journals (paywalled), Twitter (ephemeral), Discord (unsearchable), lab notebooks (private)
- **No credibility signal online**: Reddit discussions treated same as peer-reviewed findings
- **Collaboration is broken**: Brilliant people isolated; open problems go unsolved; replication attempts invisible
- **Attention is collapsing**: infinite-scroll feeds train fragmented focus and reactive thinking

### **Solution (Scholr)**
- Claims are **structured objects** with evidence grades, not just posts
- Every researcher gets a **calibration reputation score** (do your assessments age well?)
- **Open problems marketplace** links scholars with collaborators + bounties
- **Replications tracked**: Failed experiments earn credit, not shame
- **Moderation is transparent**: All decisions + rationale public + reviewable

### **Why Now**
- **AI era demands trust**: LLMs hallucinate; humans need credible sources. Scholr is the source of truth layer.
- **Replication crisis peak**: Nature/Science finally funding reproducibility initiatives. Scholr captures that demand.
- **Collaboration tech matured**: Slack, GitHub showed how to coordinate globally. Academic teams ready for it.
- **Researcher burnout**: PhD students want *impact*, not just publications. Scholr ties impact to reputation directly.

### **Market**
- **TAM**: ~8M active researchers globally + 23M students in STEM
- **SAM** (reachable): Top 100 universities + institutes = ~500K early adopters
- **Unit economics**: Post-doc salary = $60–80K; if even 1% of SAM pays $10/mo, that's $50M ARR potential

### **Differentiator**
- *Nobody else* pairs social (discovery) + reputation (credibility) + collaboration (execution)
- Competitors: ResearchGate (no trust layer), Slack (no research context), journals (slow + paywalled)
- Scholr = **the only platform where evidence quality matters more than viral reach**

### **Traction**
- Alpha: Live at [scholr-beryl.vercel.app](scholr-beryl.vercel.app)
- Features: Posts, topics, voting, messaging, moderation, RBAC
- Next 12 weeks: Claims with evidence grades + open problem marketplace

### **Financials** (if asked)
- **Cost to run**: ~$50/mo (Vercel + Render + Supabase)
- **Team**: 2 full-time engineers
- **Runway**: Self-funded; seeking $500K seed for hiring (2 more engineers, 1 PM/Head of Science)

## 🚀 **Launch Strategy** (First 90 Days)

**Week 1-2: Soft Launch**
- 10 alpha users (personally recruited researchers)
- Feedback sprint
- Fix obvious UX bugs

**Week 3-4: Academic Communities**
- Post on r/science, r/machinelearning
- Email 50 lab leads (Cold outreach + warm intros)
- Launch HN + ProductHunt post: *"We're building GitHub for research papers"*

**Week 5-6: First Evidence Grading**
- Launch evidence quality labels
- 20 moderators trained
- Announce: *"Your research now gets graded by experts"*

**Week 7-8: Open Problems MVP**
- Launch: Post unsolved problems + find teams
- Target: 5 problems with 3+ collaborators each
- Case study: *"How team found 2 researchers to solve their Graph NN bottleneck"*

**Week 9-12: Scale + Iterate**
- 500 active users target
- First publication from collaboration
- Funding closed
