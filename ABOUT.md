# **About Scholr**

---

## **Mission**
Defend attention and make the scholarly record transparent, collaborative, and trustworthy.

We're building the operating system for research communities where deep work wins: every claim is traceable, every contributor is credited, and every open problem can form a team.

---

## **What We Do**

Scholr is an academic platform combining three levers:

Before anything else, Scholr is a direct response to modern social media design: infinite feeds optimize for compulsion, not comprehension. We optimize for comprehension.

**1. Evidence-First Discourse**
Every claim you post includes evidence quality labels (peer-reviewed, preprint, data, anecdotal, opinion). Community votes don't just score popularity—they calibrate credibility. Over time, we track whose assessments age best.

**2. Collaboration Infrastructure**
Post an open problem. Get auto-matched with researchers. Form a team. Track progress. Share credit. All within research context. No more hunting for collaborators in Twitter threads.

**3. Transparent Moderation**
Moderation happens *in public*. Every decision logged, every rationale visible, every user can appeal. Safety without secrecy.

---

## **Why Scholr?**

### **Problem We Solve**

Research productivity is collapsing:
- **70% of published findings fail to replicate** (but society keeps citing them)
- **Knowledge is fragmented** across journals (paywalled), Twitter (ephemeral), Slack (unsearchable), notebooks (private)
- **No credibility signal**: A Reddit post gets the same visibility as a Nature paper
- **Collaboration is broken**: Open problems unsolved; brilliant people isolated; silos everywhere
- **Replications are invisible**: Failed experiments earn shame, not credit

### **Our Approach**

Claims are **first-class objects** with evidence grades, not just posts.
Reputation is **earned through calibration** (do your assessments age well?), not followers.
Collaboration is **infrastructure**, not an afterthought.
Replications are **tracked and credited**.
Moderation is **transparent and reversible**.

Attention is treated as a **resource to protect**, not a metric to exploit.

## **Product Highlights (MVP)**

✅ **Claim posting** with topics and references  
✅ **Upvote/downvote** with confidence scoring

✅ **Comments & discussions** on claims

✅ **Admin moderation** with reporting & banning

🔗 **Contradiction detection** (link conflicting claims)
